Giraffes

This website will explain to you why and how giraffes are such wonderful creatures.

Eating Habits

Giraffes are highly adaptable when it comes to their diets. Evidence shows that their dietary preferences often vary depending on the species of plants available in the specific region and climate in which they live. In some parts of South and East Africa, giraffes feed on deciduous trees, shrubs and vines during the wet season, and on evergreen species, near streams and rivers, during the dry season. Giraffes use their dexterous and long tongues as well as the ridged roof of their mouths to help feed on a variety of leaves and shoots.

Sleeping Habits

Occasionally, and only for very short periods of no more than 5 minutes, giraffes can sleep with their head resting on their back (see below). But this puts the animals in an extremely vulnerable position, hence the brevity and rarity. Usually, giraffes rest while standing up.

Habitation

Giraffe habitat consists mainly of savanna, sahel, and dry open woodland. Treeless grasslands with low vegetation are not ideal for giraffes because it's hard for them to reach down far enough to eat off the ground.
